Algae

Astaxanthin is a natural red pigment found in certain algae and tiny organisms in the ocean. It’s the same nutrient that makes animals like shrimp, krill, and even flamingos turn pink or red. When Sea Monkeys (which are a type of brine shrimp) eat small amounts of astaxanthin as part of their diet, the pigment can build up in their bodies. This can give them a reddish or pinkish tint, especially as they grow larger. Astaxanthin doesn’t dye them instantly—rather, it’s a natural color enhancer that shows up over time as they eat foods that contain it. This color change is harmless and is similar to what happens to shrimp and other small aquatic animals in the wild.

Because astaxanthin is very strong, it’s usually used only in tiny amounts, mixed into specially prepared foods. It’s meant as a color supplement, not their main food source.
